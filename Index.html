UNPLUG TO LEVEL UP

THE PROBLEM

In the current generation, digital devices are the lifeline of all people, of all countries, in most everyday activities. However, as a result of this, many people have bee spending most of their lives in front of screens, and many feel that they will simply not be able to survive without them. As a result of this technological frenzy, many people experienced decline in productivity, an increase in digital burnout, and even a sharp decrease in social interaction.

Why This Is A Serious Issue For The Current Generation

The current generation, "Gen Alpha", has grown up completely surrounded by the digital world. According to sources, 40% have a tablet by age 2, 58% have one by age 4 and almost one in four kids have a personal cell phone by age 8. Social Media outlets, notouriously TikTok and Instagram, have been the main cause of the rise of short-form video content.

How Short Form Media and "Brainrot" Is Destroying The Younger Generation, and Others Too

The term brainrot has changed drastically in meaning from the 2020s. Short form content, especially brainrot, has been a big cause of social media addiction, general screen addiction, reduced attention span and even anxiety and depression. Brainrot has now come to mean any type of nonsensical and senseless content in the internet, that also causes some extent of screen addiction and reduced attention spans. Now, even adults, especially young working gen-z adults are also finding screen addiction, brainrot and "doomscrolling" (Mindless scrolling of short-form content) to be a prevalent problem in their daily lives hampering their productivity and mental capacity.

Characteristics of Screen Addiction

These are the most prevalent signs of Screen/Gadget Addiction:

  • Being uninterested and unmotivated to achieve one's dreams.
  • Seeing gadgets and the online world as the only source of happiness.
  • Feeling the need to use your phone constantly even during parties or entertaining events.
  • Feeling uninterested to do even your favourite offline activities(like playing your favourite sport).
  • Feeling extremely upset when you cannot access your digital devices but feel better when you are allowed to.

THE SOLUTION

In order to understand your screentime habits and patterns, we have created a self-assessment tool that you can fill out. This assessment will help you understand your screen usage patterns and also identify whether you are at risk of screen addiction, or even, if you are currently addicted. Click on the self-assessment button on the side-panel to the left to take the assessment.

We have also created a page for parents who wish to restrict the screentime of their child, and give them tips on how to manage their screen overuse.
